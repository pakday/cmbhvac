Trustworthy Air Conditioning Installation

Central air conditioners cool your home by cycling warm indoor air through a closed-loop system. Here’s the basic process:

    Warm indoor air is pulled into return ducts and passed over an evaporator coil filled with refrigerant.
    The refrigerant absorbs heat from the air, turning into a gas.
    That gas is pumped to the outdoor condenser unit, where heat is released and the refrigerant cools back into a liquid.
    Cooled air is pushed back into your home through ducts and vents, lowering indoor temperatures.

Important Factors to Consider When Installing a Brand New Air Conditioner

Installing a new AC system is an investment in your home’s comfort. Here are the top factors our team considers during every installation:
Size & Capacity

A system that’s too small cannot cool efficiently. One that’s too large will cycle on and off constantly, wasting energy and wearing out faster. Proper sizing based on your home’s square footage and layout is essential to the longevity usage of your system.
Efficiency

Look for models with high SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) ratings. The higher the rating, the more energy-efficient the unit. Our technicians offer superior air conditioning services and will help you evaluate long-term energy savings when comparing systems.
Operational Noise

Some homeowners prefer quieter systems, especially for bedrooms or home offices. Modern AC units offer varying noise levels, and we’ll walk you through which models meet your sound sensitivity needs.
Reliability

Our team recommends equipment from manufacturers with a strong reputation for durability and easy serviceability. We also help you understand manufacturer warranties and the options for ongoing care.

What to Know About Regular Air Conditioner Maintenance

Routine AC maintenance includes multiple checkpoints to ensure your system operates efficiently and safely. During each visit, our team typically performs:

    Thermostat Testing: We verify that your thermostat reads accurately and communicates with your system correctly.

    Air Filter Inspection and Replacement: Dirty filters restrict airflow, which can lead to poor performance and unnecessary strain on your equipment.

    Evaporator and Condenser Coil Cleaning: Dust and debris reduce cooling power. Cleaning coils improves heat transfer and overall efficiency.

    Refrigerant Level Checks: We inspect refrigerant lines for leaks and ensure the system has the proper charge.

    Electrical Connections: Loose or damaged wiring can lead to system failure or pose safety hazards. We check all connections and controls.

    Blower Motor Assessment: If your blower fan is slow or unresponsive, airflow suffers. We ensure that motors and belts are in good working condition.

Annual tune-ups help prevent mid-season breakdowns and allow small issues to be corrected before they become costly repairs. Most major manufacturers also require regular service to maintain warranty coverage.
